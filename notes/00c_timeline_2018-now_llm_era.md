# The LLM Era (2018-Now)

> [Previous: Resurrection & Revolution](00b_timeline_1986-2017_resurrection_and_revolution.md) | [Back to Timeline Hub](00_timeline.md)

| Year | What | Who | Key Idea |
|------|------|-----|----------|
| 2018 | **ELMo** | Peters et al. (AllenNLP / AI2) | "Embeddings from Language Models" — generated word embeddings using a deep bidirectional LSTM pretrained on large text. Unlike Word2Vec/GloVe (one vector per word), ELMo produces *different* vectors depending on context ("bank" in "river bank" vs. "bank account"). The first major contextualized embedding. Proved that pretraining on language modeling transfers to downstream tasks |
| 2018 | **ULMFiT** | Howard & Ruder (fast.ai) | "Universal Language Model Fine-tuning for Text Classification" — showed that a language model pretrained on general text could be fine-tuned on small task-specific datasets with remarkable results. Introduced discriminative fine-tuning (different learning rates per layer) and gradual unfreezing. Alongside ELMo, established the pretrain → fine-tune paradigm that BERT and GPT perfected |
| 2018 | **BERT** | Devlin et al. (Google) | Encoder-only transformer, pretrained with masked language modeling. Successor to ELMo (2018) — replaced biLSTMs with Transformer self-attention. `TransformerTranslator` is this style |
| 2018 | **GPT-1** | Radford et al. (OpenAI) | Decoder-only transformer, autoregressive pretraining on BookCorpus |
| 2018 | **StyleGAN** | Karras, Laine, Aila (NVIDIA) | Generated photorealistic human faces so convincing they spawned thispersondoesnotexist.com — the moment the public realized AI could create people who never existed. Used progressive growing (start at 4×4 resolution, gradually increase) and style mixing at different layers. Cultural shock and deepfake anxiety followed |
| 2018 | **Gender Shades** | Buolamwini & Gebru (MIT Media Lab) | Audited commercial facial recognition systems (Microsoft, IBM, Face++) and found error rates up to 34.7% for dark-skinned women vs. 0.8% for light-skinned men. Forced all three companies to improve their systems and catalyzed the AI ethics movement. Timnit Gebru went on to co-author the Stochastic Parrots paper (2021), warning about large language model risks |
| 2018 | **Mixed-precision training** | Micikevicius et al. (NVIDIA/Baidu) | Training neural networks using half-precision (FP16) arithmetic with key operations in full precision (FP32). Nearly halved memory usage and doubled throughput on NVIDIA Volta GPUs with minimal accuracy loss. Combined with loss scaling to prevent gradient underflow. Became standard practice — virtually all large model training after 2018 uses mixed precision |
| 2019 | **GPT-2** | OpenAI | 1.5B params. Showed scaling up decoder-only transformers yields strong zero-shot performance |
| 2019 | **T5** | Google | "Text-to-Text Transfer Transformer" — frames every NLP task as text-in, text-out |
| 2019 | **Megatron-LM** | Shoeybi et al. (NVIDIA) | Model parallelism for training massive transformers — split individual layers across multiple GPUs (tensor parallelism). Trained an 8.3B parameter transformer, the largest at the time. The Megatron approach (combined with DeepSpeed's data/pipeline parallelism) became the standard recipe for training 100B+ parameter models |
| 2019 | **Model Cards** | Mitchell et al. (Google) | "Model Cards for Model Reporting" — proposed standardized documentation for ML models, including intended use, performance across demographic groups, and ethical considerations. A practical response to the bias revelations of 2016-2018 (COMPAS, Gender Shades). Now adopted by Hugging Face, Google, and others as standard practice |
| 2019 | **AlphaStar** | Vinyals et al. (DeepMind) | Grandmaster-level StarCraft II — real-time, imperfect information, multi-unit coordination. Top 0.2% of human players |
| 2019 | **OpenAI Five** | OpenAI | Beat world champions at Dota 2 (5v5). Trained with PPO — the same algorithm later used for RLHF in ChatGPT |
| 2019 | **Score matching** | Song & Ermon (Stanford) | "Generative Modeling by Estimating Gradients of the Data Distribution" — instead of learning p(x) directly, learn the *gradient* of log p(x) (the "score function") and follow it to generate data. Unified denoising autoencoders (2008), Boltzmann machines (1985), and noise-based generation into one framework. The theoretical foundation that made DDPM (2020) possible |
| 2020 | **GPT-3** | OpenAI | 175B params. In-context learning — few-shot via prompting, no fine-tuning needed |
| 2020 | **AlphaFold 2** | Jumper et al. (DeepMind) | Solved protein folding — predicted 3D protein structures with atomic accuracy. RL + attention. Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2024 |
| 2020 | **DDPM** | Ho, Jain, Abbeel (UC Berkeley) | "Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models" (NeurIPS 2020). Denoising Diffusion — gradually add Gaussian noise, train a network to reverse it one step at a time. **Simplified Sohl-Dickstein's 2015 framework** by dropping the full variational lower bound in favor of plain MSE between true and predicted noise, which made the whole thing trainable in PyTorch without any special machinery. Stable training (no adversarial game), sharp outputs, essentially no mode collapse. The same "corrupt then reconstruct" idea as denoising autoencoders (2008), scaled to thousands of noise steps. Inherited the reparameterization trick from VAE (2013). This was the technical foundation; Dhariwal & Nichol's 2021 follow-up is what actually dethroned GANs |
| 2020 | **Vision Transformer (ViT)** | Dosovitskiy et al. (Google) | Applied transformers to images by splitting into patches. Transformers escape NLP |
| 2020 | **SimCLR** | Chen et al. (Google Brain) | "Simple Framework for Contrastive Learning" — learned visual representations without labels by pulling augmented views of the same image together and pushing different images apart. Showed that self-supervised pretraining could match supervised performance on ImageNet with enough compute. Key insight: strong data augmentation + large batch sizes + a simple projection head = powerful representations |
| 2020 | **MoCo** | He et al. (Facebook AI) | Momentum Contrast — self-supervised visual learning using a momentum-updated encoder and a queue of negative examples. More memory-efficient than SimCLR (didn't need giant batch sizes). Kaiming He (also behind ResNet) showed that contrastive self-supervision could scale efficiently. MoCo and SimCLR together launched the self-supervised vision revolution |
| 2020 | **wav2vec 2.0** | Baevski et al. (Facebook AI) | Self-supervised speech representation learning — masks portions of raw audio and trains the model to identify the correct latent representations (contrastive learning, like masked language modeling but for speech). Fine-tuned with just 10 minutes of labeled data to match fully supervised systems trained on 100x more. Applied the pretrain → fine-tune paradigm from NLP to speech |
| 2020 | **DeepSpeed / ZeRO** | Rajbhandari et al. (Microsoft) | Zero Redundancy Optimizer — partitioned model states (parameters, gradients, optimizer states) across GPUs instead of replicating them. Enabled training models with trillions of parameters. Combined with Megatron-LM's tensor parallelism to create the Megatron-DeepSpeed framework used to train many of the largest language models |
| 2021 | **Diffusion Beats GANs** | Dhariwal & Nichol (OpenAI) | "Diffusion Models Beat GANs on Image Synthesis" (NeurIPS 2021). The paper that did what its title says — showed diffusion models beating BigGAN on ImageNet at comparable compute budgets. This was the moment the ML community collectively stopped betting on GANs for high-stakes generative work and started betting on diffusion. Built directly on Ho et al.'s 2020 DDPM. Dhariwal later co-founded Anthropic; Nichol was a key contributor on the DALL-E series at OpenAI. The inflection point — every image-generation startup founded after 2021 has been diffusion-based, not GAN-based |
| 2021 | **Variational Diffusion Models** | Kingma, Salimans, Poole, Ho | Reframed diffusion models as hierarchical VAEs with a fixed (not learned) encoder and T latent layers. Diederik Kingma — co-author of the 2013 VAE paper — returning to the diffusion lineage and explicitly closing the VAE ↔ DDPM loop mathematically. Showed that DDPM, VAE, and score-matching are all views of the same underlying object. Also improved training via a continuous-time noise schedule. Bridges 00b's VAE (2013) and 00c's DDPM (2020) into one family tree |
| 2021 | **DALL-E** | OpenAI | Transformer generates images from text descriptions |
| 2021 | **DINO** | Caron et al. (Facebook AI) | Self-Distillation with No Labels — a Vision Transformer trained with self-supervised distillation (student-teacher with no labels) spontaneously learned to segment objects and attend to semantic parts. The attention maps were strikingly interpretable — the model "understood" object boundaries without ever being told what objects are. Showed that ViTs + self-supervision discover visual structure that CNNs missed |
| 2022 | **ChatGPT / InstructGPT** | OpenAI | RLHF (reinforcement learning from human feedback) using PPO (Schulman, 2017) aligns GPT-3.5 to follow instructions |
| 2022 | **Stable Diffusion** | Rombach et al. | Latent diffusion — run denoising in compressed latent space, not pixel space. Open-sourced, triggered the AI art explosion |
| 2022 | **Chinchilla** | Hoffmann et al. (DeepMind) | Scaling laws — models were over-parameterized & under-trained. Train longer on more data |
| 2022 | **Whisper** | Radford et al. (OpenAI) | Large-scale weakly supervised speech recognition — trained on 680,000 hours of multilingual audio from the internet. Robust to accents, background noise, and technical language. Open-sourced. The same Alec Radford behind GPT-1/GPT-2 applied the "scale + diverse data" philosophy to ASR, achieving near-human accuracy across 97 languages |
| 2023 | **GPT-4** | OpenAI | Multimodal (text + vision), massive leap in reasoning |
| 2023 | **LLaMA** | Meta | Open-weight LLMs. Kicked off the open-source LLM explosion |
| 2023 | **Mixture of Experts (MoE)** | Mistral, Google | Only activate a subset of parameters per token — scale without scaling compute linearly |
| 2023 | **SeamlessM4T** | Meta AI | "Massively Multilingual & Multimodal Machine Translation" — a single model handling speech-to-speech, speech-to-text, text-to-speech, and text-to-text translation across 100+ languages. Built on wav2vec 2.0 (2020) for speech and NLLB (2022) for text. Meta's culmination of the Facebook Translation research line that started with ConvS2S (2017) |
| 2024 | **State-space models (Mamba)** | Gu & Dao | Alternative to attention — linear-time sequence modeling, challenging transformer dominance for long sequences |
| 2024 | **EU AI Act** | European Parliament | First comprehensive AI regulation — classifies AI systems by risk level (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal) with corresponding obligations. Bans social scoring and real-time biometric surveillance (with exceptions). High-risk systems require conformity assessments, transparency, and human oversight. The regulatory response to a decade of bias revelations (COMPAS 2016, Gender Shades 2018) and generative AI concerns |
| 2024-25 | **Reasoning models** | OpenAI (o1/o3), Anthropic (Claude), DeepSeek (R1) | Chain-of-thought at inference time. Models "think" before answering |
