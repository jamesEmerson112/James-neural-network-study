# John McCarthy — The Man Who Named AI and Built Its First Language

> *Last updated: 2026-03-15*

## The Origin Story

- Born September 4, 1927, Boston, Massachusetts
- **Communist parents** — dad was a carpenter/union organizer/inventor, mom was a Lithuanian Jewish suffrage activist
- Sickly child who turned to books. Family moved to Los Angeles hoping the climate would help
- **Taught himself college math from Caltech textbooks as a teenager**
- Got into Caltech, skipped 2 years of math — but **failed physical education** and had to serve in the U.S. Army before being readmitted
- B.S. in Mathematics from Caltech (1948)
- PhD in Mathematics from Princeton (1951) — dissertation on partial differential equations

## Career: The Grand Tour

| Where | When | What He Did |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Princeton | 1951-53 | Post-doc in math |
| Dartmouth | 1955-58 | **Coined "AI" (1956)**, organized the Dartmouth Workshop |
| MIT | 1956-62 | **Invented LISP (1958)**, co-founded MIT AI Lab with Minsky, pioneered **time-sharing** |
| Stanford | 1962-2011 | **Founded SAIL** (Stanford AI Lab, 1963), stayed nearly 40 years |

## His Inventions (This Is Insane)

### 1. Coined "Artificial Intelligence" (1955-56)
- Wrote the Dartmouth Workshop proposal with Minsky, Shannon, Rochester
- Deliberately chose "AI" to separate from "cybernetics" and "automata theory"
- A branding move that defined an entire field

### 2. LISP (1958)
- Created at MIT on the IBM 704
- Wanted a language for symbolic reasoning, not number crunching
- Based on **lambda calculus** (Church), not von Neumann architecture
- His student Steve Russell implemented the `eval` function — McCarthy was surprised it could actually work
- Dominated AI programming for 30 years
- See: [lisp_deep_dive.md](lisp_deep_dive.md) for full analysis

### 3. Garbage Collection (1959)
- Invented automatic memory management (mark-and-sweep algorithm) for LISP
- Now standard in every modern managed language (Java, Python, Go, JavaScript)
- Millions of developers use this daily without knowing McCarthy invented it

### 4. Time-Sharing (1959)
- First to describe general-purpose computer time-sharing (memo dated January 1, 1959)
- Multiple users sharing one computer simultaneously
- Enabled ARPANET development
- Foundation for all modern multi-user computing

### 5. Utility Computing Vision (1961)
- Predicted computing would be sold like electricity — "computing as a utility"
- This is **cloud computing**, predicted 50 years early (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)

### 6. Situation Calculus (1963)
- Formal logic for reasoning about actions and change
- Used in robotics and planning systems
- Foundation for logical AI approaches

## The Stanford AI Lab (SAIL)

- Founded 1963 with Les Earnest
- McCarthy directed 1965-1980
- One of the world's premier AI research centers
- Pioneered robotics, NLP, computer vision
- Produced generations of AI researchers
- Closed 1980, reopened 2004

## Personal Life — The Wild Parts

- **Rock climber, pilot, skydiver** — despite being a sickly kid
- Students called him **"Uncle John"** at MIT
- Had a habit of answering questions **days later** in hallways with "robotically precise diction"
- Second wife **Vera Watson** — computer programmer AND world-class mountaineer — **died in 1978 climbing Annapurna** in an all-women Himalayan expedition
- **Political transformation**: raised by communists → visited Soviet-occupied Czechoslovakia in 1968 → became a **conservative Republican**
- One of the first to propose the Bill of Rights should guarantee **digital privacy rights**

## Awards

- **Turing Award (1971)** — the "Nobel Prize of computing"
- **National Medal of Science (1990)**
- **Kyoto Prize (1988)**
- National Academy of Sciences, National Academy of Engineering
- Computer History Museum Fellow (1999)

## Death

- October 24, 2011, age 84, at Stanford. Pulmonary hypertension.
- Died having witnessed AI grow from a summer workshop idea to one of the most transformative forces in society

## His AI Prediction

Bet it would take about **50 years** to achieve human-level AI (from the 1960s). That puts it around 2010-2015. GPT and modern LLMs arrived within a decade of that window.

## The Irony

McCarthy championed **logic-based symbolic AI** his whole career. The approach that actually scaled was **connectionism** (neural nets) — the thing his collaborator Minsky tried to kill. McCarthy's tools (LISP, garbage collection, time-sharing) ended up powering the rival paradigm.
